# Liquid Biopsy Technology

## What is Liquid Biopsy?

Liquid biopsy is a technology for diagnosing diseases by detecting biomarkers in body fluids such as blood, urine, and cerebrospinal fluid. Compared with traditional tissue biopsy, it has the advantages of minimally invasive, repeatable sampling, and dynamic monitoring.

## Main Biomarkers of Liquid Biopsy

| Biomarker | Source | Application |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Circulating Tumor DNA (ctDNA) | Tumor cell apoptosis | Cancer screening, efficacy monitoring |
| Circulating Tumor Cells (CTC) | Tumor cell shedding | Cancer staging, prognosis assessment |
| Exosomes | Various cells | Multiple disease diagnosis |
| MicroRNA (miRNA) | Various cells | Disease biomarkers |

## Application Areas

### 1. Cancer Early Screening

Liquid biopsy can detect tumor signals in early-stage cancer:

- **Early Detection**: Detecting tumors earlier than imaging
- **High-risk Population Screening**: Suitable for cancer high-risk groups
- **Dynamic Monitoring**: Tracking tumor development

### 2. Efficacy Monitoring

Evaluating treatment effects through dynamic changes in ctDNA:

- Assessing whether treatment is effective
- Early detection of drug resistance
- Guiding subsequent treatment strategies

### 3. Recurrence Monitoring

Regular post-surgery testing to monitor tumor recurrence:

- Detecting recurrence earlier than imaging
- Timely adjustment of treatment strategies

## Technical Progress

### Single-cell Sequencing

Single-cell liquid biopsy technology can analyze the characteristics of individual cells.

### Multi-omics Integration

Integrating genomic, epigenomic, and proteomic data.
