# Data Analysis and Research Methods

This topic trains graph reading, experimental reasoning, scientific writing, and oral explanation. It is the final step from “knowing” to “scoring well.”

## Knowledge Checklist

- Interpreting line, bar, scatter, and flow charts
- Mean, error, extremes, and trend analysis
- Correlation, causation, and basics of hypothesis testing
- Research question, hypothesis, variable, and conclusion writing
- Argument structure, lab reports, and answer format

## Typical Question

Question: Two curves separate clearly after 12 hours in an experiment. How can you judge whether the difference comes from the treatment?

How to solve: Check experimental design, statistical significance, variable control, and mechanism explanation.

## Common Pitfalls

- Treating correlation as causation
- Ignoring sample size and error bars
- Writing conclusions too absolutely
- Describing only observations without mechanisms

## Self-check Quiz

1. What can error bars tell you?
2. What is the difference between “supporting a hypothesis” and “proving a hypothesis”?
3. What should a strong conclusion contain?
4. How should you reason when data are unfamiliar?
