# Plant Physiology

This topic covers plant nutrition, transport, growth, development, and stress responses. It is often tested through integrated reasoning.

## Knowledge Checklist

- Water absorption, mineral uptake, and transport
- Photosynthesis and assimilate allocation
- Plant hormones and their interactions
- Phototropism, gravitropism, and apical dominance
- Flowering, fruit development, and seed germination
- Stress responses and tolerance mechanisms

## Typical Question

Question: Why does removing the shoot apex promote lateral bud growth, and why can auxin spraying partially reverse that effect?

How to solve: Explain apical dominance, polar auxin transport, and concentration-dependent effects.

## Common Pitfalls

- Treating auxin as always growth-promoting
- Confusing transpiration with root pressure
- Ignoring interactions between environmental factors and hormones
- Assuming all hormones are synthesized in one site only

## Self-check Quiz

1. Why does transpiration help water transport?
2. Why do plant hormones often interact synergistically or antagonistically?
3. How does photoperiod relate to flowering?
4. How do plants respond to drought or salt stress?
