# Molecular Genetics

This topic connects inheritance patterns with molecular mechanisms and is one of the most differentiating areas in competitions.

## Knowledge Checklist

- DNA, RNA, and information flow
- Replication, transcription, translation, and gene regulation
- Mutation, recombination, rearrangement, and variation
- Mendelian laws, linkage, crossing-over, and pedigree analysis
- Sex-linked inheritance, quantitative traits, and polygenic inheritance
- Molecular markers, sequencing, and basic gene editing concepts

## Typical Question

Question: A mutation causes a protein to stop early, but the mRNA length remains unchanged. What type of mutation is most likely?

How to solve: Consider nonsense mutation, codon changes, and reading-frame stability.

## Common Pitfalls

- Being able to calculate Mendelian ratios but not explain molecular causes
- Confusing mutation with recombination
- Ignoring sex information in pedigree problems
- Thinking gene expression ends after transcription and translation

## Self-check Quiz

1. Why is DNA replication semi-conservative?
2. Where do transcription and translation occur?
3. How do you identify autosomal dominant inheritance in a pedigree?
4. Why does gene regulation affect phenotype?
