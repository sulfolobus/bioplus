# Evolution and Classification

This topic links biodiversity, phylogeny, and adaptive evolution, helping explain why so many species exist.

## Knowledge Checklist

- Natural selection, artificial selection, and sexual selection
- Adaptation, fitness, and selection pressure
- Speciation, isolation, and adaptive radiation
- Classification systems and phylogenetic trees
- Morphological, molecular, homologous, and analogous evidence

## Typical Question

Question: Why is it not enough to classify two species by appearance alone if they look similar but are not closely related?

How to solve: Discuss analogous structures, convergent evolution, and molecular evidence.

## Common Pitfalls

- Viewing evolution as a march toward “higher” organisms
- Confusing homologous and analogous structures
- Making classification decisions from a single trait only
- Mixing population-level evolution with individual-level change

## Self-check Quiz

1. Why does natural selection act on phenotype while evolution happens in populations?
2. How does reproductive isolation lead to new species?
3. Why is molecular evidence useful in classification?
4. What does adaptive radiation show?
