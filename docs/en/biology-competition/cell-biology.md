# Cell Biology

This topic covers cell structure, function, division, metabolism, and microscopy-based analysis. It is one of the most foundational and frequently tested areas in biology competitions.

## Knowledge Checklist

- Cell theory and basic properties of cells
- Prokaryotic, eukaryotic, and specialized cells
- Cell membrane, organelles, nucleus, and membrane systems
- Membrane transport, endocytosis, and exocytosis
- Cell cycle, mitosis, and meiosis basics
- Microscopy, image interpretation, and experimental design

## Typical Question

Question: A cell can perform photosynthesis and aerobic respiration. What type of cell might it be, and why?

How to solve: Identify structural clues such as chloroplasts, mitochondria, nucleus, and cell wall, then connect structure with function.

## Common Pitfalls

- Confusing prokaryotic cells with acellular organisms
- Memorizing organelle functions in isolation without system links
- Ignoring chromosome behavior when interpreting division diagrams
- Drawing conclusions without considering variable control

## Self-check Quiz

1. Why is the cell membrane selectively permeable?
2. What special roles do mitochondria and chloroplasts play?
3. How can you tell which stage of mitosis a cell is in from a diagram?
4. Why is the cell cycle such a frequent competition topic?
