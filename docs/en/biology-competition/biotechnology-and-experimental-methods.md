# Biotechnology and Experimental Methods

This topic focuses on experimental design, molecular detection, operation standards, and result interpretation. It turns knowledge into points.

## Knowledge Checklist

- Microscope use, centrifugation, pipetting, and aseptic technique
- PCR, gel electrophoresis, sequencing, and molecular probes
- ELISA, hybridization, markers, and tracing methods
- Experimental design, control groups, and error analysis
- Graph reading, statistics, and result reporting

## Typical Question

Question: How can gel electrophoresis be used to judge whether PCR amplification succeeded?

How to solve: Combine band position, fragment size, positive/negative controls, and possible contamination.

## Common Pitfalls

- Looking only at result bands and ignoring controls
- Treating failure as merely “careless operation”
- Ignoring non-specific bands and blank lanes
- Reporting only averages without variation

## Self-check Quiz

1. Why are both negative and positive controls necessary?
2. What is the role of each PCR component?
3. How do you decide whether data support a hypothesis?
4. Why is contamination control so important in molecular experiments?
