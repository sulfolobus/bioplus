# Biochemistry

This topic focuses on biomolecular structure, energy transfer, metabolic pathways, and enzyme principles. It requires strong conceptual understanding and reasoning.

## Knowledge Checklist

- Structure-function relationships of amino acids, proteins, and enzymes
- Chemical basics of carbohydrates, lipids, and nucleic acids
- ATP, NADH, FADH2, and energy currency concepts
- Cellular respiration, photosynthesis, and metabolic coupling
- Enzyme kinetics, inhibition, denaturation, and regulation
- Feedback control and rate-limiting steps in pathways

## Typical Question

Question: Why does increasing temperature speed up an enzymatic reaction within a certain range, but slow it down at higher levels?

How to solve: Explain collision frequency first, then describe protein denaturation and active-site disruption.

## Common Pitfalls

- Treating ATP as long-term energy storage
- Ignoring the relationship between enzyme structure and function
- Memorizing equations without understanding metabolic flow
- Confusing how substrate concentration, enzyme concentration, temperature, and pH act differently

3. How are carbon fixation and energy conversion coupled in photosynthesis?
