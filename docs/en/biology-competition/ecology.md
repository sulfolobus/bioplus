# Ecology

This topic treats organisms and environments as dynamic systems and emphasizes population dynamics, communities, energy flow, and ecological modeling.

## Knowledge Checklist

- Population growth models and density dependence
- Community structure, species interactions, and succession
- Ecosystem components, food webs, and trophic levels
- Energy flow, matter cycling, and ecological efficiency
- Niche, edge effect, and biodiversity
- Restoration, conservation, and management

## Typical Question

Question: A lake suffers from eutrophication and frequent algal blooms. What ecological mechanisms may be involved?

How to solve: Analyze nutrient input, producer boom, oxygen decline, community imbalance, and feedback loops.

## Common Pitfalls

- Equating more producers with a more stable ecosystem
- Confusing ecosystem openness with matter cycling
- Knowing trophic levels but not being able to calculate energy transfer
- Ignoring time scale and spatial scale

## Self-check Quiz

1. How do intrinsic growth rate and carrying capacity shape population curves?
2. Why is energy flow one-way?
3. When do primary and secondary succession occur?
4. Why is biodiversity linked to ecosystem stability?
