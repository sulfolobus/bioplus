# Hypertension Research Progress - Western and Chinese Medicine

Hypertension (high blood pressure) is one of the most common cardiovascular diseases, divided into primary hypertension and secondary hypertension.

## Disease Definition

### Blood Pressure Classification

| Classification | Systolic (mmHg) | Diastolic (mmHg) |
|---------------|------------------|------------------|
| Normal | <120 | <80 |
| Elevated | 120-129 | <80 |
| Hypertension Stage 1 | 130-139 | 80-89 |
| Hypertension Stage 2 | ≥140 | ≥90 |
| Hypertensive Crisis | ≥180 | ≥120 |

## Classification

### Primary Hypertension

- Accounts for 90-95%
- Unknown cause
- Multiple factors (genetics, environment, lifestyle)
- Lifelong condition

### Secondary Hypertension

- Accounts for 5-10%
- Clear cause
- Common causes: kidney disease, endocrine disease, vascular disease
- Potentially curable

## Western Medicine Treatment

### Drug Therapy

#### First-line Drugs

| Drug Category | Representative Drug | Mechanism |
|--------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Calcium Channel Blockers | Amlodipine | Vasodilation |
| ACE Inhibitors | Benazepril | Inhibit vasoconstriction |
| ARB | Valsartan | Block angiotensin |
| Diuretics | Hydrochlorothiazide | Reduce blood volume |
| Beta Blockers | Metoprolol | Reduce heart rate |

#### Newer Drugs

- **Sacubitril Valsartan**: Approved for both heart failure and hypertension
- **Aprepitent**: New option for resistant hypertension
- **Baxdrostat**: New drug for primary aldosteronism
- **Ambrisentan**: New mechanism for resistant hypertension

### Non-drug Therapy

- Lifestyle intervention (DASH diet)
- Exercise therapy
- Weight management
- Quit smoking, limit alcohol
- Stress management

### Interventional Therapy

- **Renal Artery Denervation**: New option for resistant hypertension
- **Carotid Sinus Stimulator**: New method for blood pressure control

## Prevention and Management

### Lifestyle

| Factor | Recommendation |
|--------|----------------|
| Sodium intake | <5g/day |
| Exercise | 150 minutes/week |
| Weight | BMI 18.5-24.9 |
| Alcohol | Men <25g/day, Women <15g/day |
| Diet | DASH diet, Mediterranean diet |

### Self-monitoring

- Home blood pressure monitoring
- 24-hour ambulatory blood pressure monitoring
- Smart device assistance

### Long-term Management

- Regular follow-up
- Compliance education
- Complication screening
