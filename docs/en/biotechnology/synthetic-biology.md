# Synthetic Biology

## What is Synthetic Biology?

Synthetic biology is the discipline of designing and constructing new biological systems or redesigning existing biological systems, hailed as "the engine of the future bioeconomy".

## Application Areas of Synthetic Biology

### 1. Healthcare

| Application | Examples |
|-------------|----------|
| Cell Therapy | Engineered immune cells |
| Vaccines | Synthetic vaccines |
| Diagnostics | Biosensors |

### 2. Industrial Manufacturing

- **Biomanufacturing**: Using microorganisms to produce chemicals
- **Alternative Feedstocks**: Using renewable raw materials
- **Green Processes**: Reducing pollution

### 3. Agriculture

- **Crop Improvement**: Increasing yield and resistance
- **Synthetic Nitrogen Fixation**: Reducing fertilizer use
- **Animal Health**: Novel veterinary drugs

### 4. Environment

- **Biodegradation**: Plastic degradation
- **Wastewater Treatment**: Microbial systems
- **Carbon Capture**: Synthetic biology pathways

## Key Technologies

### 1. Genetic Circuit Design

Design gene regulatory networks to achieve specific functions.

### 2. DNA Synthesis

Chemically synthesize long-chain DNA for constructing new systems.

### 3. Automation Platforms

High-throughput screening and construction platforms.
