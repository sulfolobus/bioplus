# Cell Therapy

## What is Cell Therapy?

Cell therapy is a technology that infuses or transplants living cells into patients to treat diseases. It has achieved breakthrough progress in recent years in areas such as cancer and genetic diseases.

## Main Types of Cell Therapy

### 1. CAR-T Cell Therapy

Chimeric antigen receptor T-cell (CAR-T) is the most successful cell therapy:

| Product | Target | Indication | Approval Year |
|---------|--------|------------|---------------|
| Kymriah | CD19 | B-cell Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia | 2017 |
| Yescarta | CD19 | Large B-cell Lymphoma | 2017 |
| Breyanzi | CD19 | Lymphoma | 2021 |
| Carvykti | BCMA | Multiple Myeloma | 2022 |

#### Treatment Process

1. **Collection**: Extract T cells from the patient
2. **Modification**: Genetic engineering modification in vitro
3. **Expansion**: Mass culture of CAR-T cells
4. **Infusion**: Infuse CAR-T cells back into the patient

### 2. Stem Cell Therapy

Stem cells have self-renewal and differentiation capabilities:

- **Hematopoietic Stem Cells**: Treatment of blood diseases
- **Mesenchymal Stem Cells**: Immune regulation, tissue repair
- **Embryonic Stem Cells**: Pluripotent, can differentiate into various cell types
- **iPSC**: Induced pluripotent stem cells

### 3. Other Cell Therapies

- **NK Cell Therapy**: Natural killer cells for cancer
- **γδ T Cells**: Novel immune cell therapy
- **Treg Cells**: Treatment for autoimmune diseases

## Indications

### Approved Indications

- Hematological tumors
- Certain genetic diseases
- Ophthalmic diseases

### Under Exploration

- Solid tumors
- Autoimmune diseases
- Neurological diseases

## Challenges

- **High Cost**: Complex manufacturing process
- **Side Effects**: Cytokine release syndrome
- **Scale-up**: How to produce at scale
