# Antibody Drug Advances

## What are Antibody Drugs?

Antibody drugs are medications that use antibodies produced by the immune system to treat diseases, characterized by high specificity and low toxicity.

## Types of Antibody Drugs

### 1. Monoclonal Antibodies (mAb)

Single antibodies targeting specific antigens:

| Type | Representative Drug | Indication |
|------|---------------------|------------|
| Anti-tumor | Rituximab | Lymphoma |
| Autoimmune | Adalimumab | Rheumatoid Arthritis |
| Cardiovascular | PCSK9 Inhibitors | Hyperlipidemia |

### 2. Antibody-Drug Conjugates (ADC)

Linking cytotoxic drugs to antibodies:

- **Principle**: Antibody targeting + toxin killing
- **Advantage**: Precise targeting of cancer cells
- **Representative Drug**: Trastuzumab emtansine

### 3. Bispecific Antibodies (BsAb)

Simultaneously binding to two different antigens:

- **T-cell Engagement**: Recruiting T cells to kill tumors
- **Representative Drug**: Blatumomab

## Development Trends

### 1. Antibody Engineering Optimization

- Humanized antibodies
- Miniaturized antibodies (fragment antibodies)
- Long-acting antibodies

### 2. Novel Target Discovery

- Immune checkpoint inhibitors
- Cytokine receptors
- Tumor-specific antigens

## Market Prospects

The global antibody drug market continues to grow, exceeding $300 billion in 2025.
