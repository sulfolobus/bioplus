# LNP Technology

Lipid Nanoparticles (LNPs) are one of the most successful non-viral gene delivery systems, widely used in mRNA vaccines and gene therapy.

## LNP Composition

### Four-Component System

| Component | Ratio | Function |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| Cationic lipids | 50% | Bind nucleic acids, promote endosomal escape |
| Phospholipids | 10% | Form bilayer structure |
| Cholesterol | 38.5% | Stabilize particle integrity |
| PEG lipids | 1.5% | Prolong blood circulation |

## Mechanism of Action

### Cellular Uptake Process

1. **Plasma protein binding**: ApoE-mediated hepatocyte uptake
2. **Receptor-mediated endocytosis**: LDLR-mediated
3. **Endosome maturation**: pH decreases
4. **Endosomal escape**: Cationic lipids protonate
5. **Nucleic acid release**: Enter cytoplasm

### Proton Sponge Effect

Cationic lipids become protonated in acidic environments, creating a proton sponge effect that causes endosome rupture and releases contents.

## Clinical Applications

### mRNA Vaccines

LNP-mRNA vaccines played a critical role in COVID-19 prevention:

**Success stories:**
- Pfizer-BioNTech (Comirnaty)
- Moderna (Spikevax)

**Advantages:**
- High transfection efficiency
- Good safety profile
- Scalable manufacturing

### Gene Therapy

| Therapy | Indication | Phase |
|---------|------------|-------|
| LNP-CRISPR | Liver diseases | Phase I |
| siRNA-LNP | Liver metabolic diseases | Approved |

## Optimization Strategies

### Tissue Specificity

- Modify lipid composition
- Add targeting ligands
- Adjust particle size

### Reducing Immune Activation

- Optimize PEG content
- Use degradable lipids
