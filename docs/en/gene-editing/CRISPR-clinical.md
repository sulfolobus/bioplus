# CRISPR Clinical Applications Progress

## What is CRISPR?

CRISPR (Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats) is a revolutionary gene editing technology, known as "genetic scissors." It allows scientists to precisely modify DNA sequences with advantages of simple operation, low cost, and high efficiency.

## CRISPR in Clinical Applications

### 1. Genetic Disease Treatment

CRISPR technology is being used to treat various genetic diseases:

| Disease | Treatment Progress | Research Institution |
|---------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Sickle Cell Disease | Approved for market | Vertex/CRISPR Therapeutics |
| Beta-Thalassemia | Approved for market | Vertex/CRISPR Therapeutics |
| Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy | In clinical trials | Various biotech companies |
| Cystic Fibrosis | Pre-clinical research | Various |

### 2. Cancer Treatment

CAR-T cell therapy combined with CRISPR technology:

- **CAR-T Preparation**: Using CRISPR to improve T cell efficiency
- **Universal Allogeneic CAR-T**: Solving donor source problems
- **Solid Tumor Breakthrough**: Challenges being addressed

### 3. Eye Diseases

The eye, due to its immune-privileged status, is an ideal target for CRISPR therapy:

- Leber Congenital Amaurosis
- Retinitis Pigmentosa

## Challenges and Prospects

### Current Challenges

- **Off-target effects**: Risk of editing non-target genes
- **Delivery issues**: How to efficiently deliver to target tissues
- **Long-term safety**: Durability of editing effects
