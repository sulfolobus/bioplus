# Cancer Treatment

Gene editing technology shows tremendous potential in cancer treatment, especially in CAR-T cell therapy and cancer gene therapy.

## CAR-T Cell Therapy

### Principle

CAR-T (Chimeric Antigen Receptor T-cell) therapy modifies the patient's T cells to recognize and kill cancer cells.

**Modification steps:**
1. Isolate T cells from patient blood
2. Use gene editing to introduce CAR gene
3. Amplify CAR-T cells in vitro
4. Infuse back into patient

### Clinical Data

- **Complete remission rate**: 30-50%
- **One-year progression-free survival**: 40-60%
- **Severe cytokine release syndrome**: 20-30%

## Gene Editing Enhanced Therapy

### TCR-T Therapy

Modify T cells to express tumor-specific T cell receptors.

**Advantages:**
- Recognizes more tumor antigens
- Can recognize intracellular antigens

### CRISPR-Enhanced T Cells

Using CRISPR technology:
- Knock out T cell inhibitory receptors (PD-1)
- Enhance T cell persistence
- Improve tumor infiltration

## Challenges in Solid Tumor Treatment

### Major Challenges

| Challenge | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| Tumor microenvironment | Immunosuppression |
| Antigen heterogeneity | Different antigen expression |
| T cell exhaustion | Continuous antigen stimulation |
| Off-target risk | Healthy tissue damage |

### Solutions

- Dual-target CAR-T
- Arm with immune-stimulatory molecules
- Combined with checkpoint inhibitors
