# Genetic Disease Treatment

Genetic diseases are caused by gene mutations. Gene therapy offers hope for curing these conditions.

## Common Genetic Disease Types

### Single-Gene Genetic Diseases

| Disease | Causal Gene | Prevalence |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Sickle cell anemia | HBB | 1/500 African descent |
| Cystic fibrosis | CFTR | 1/3500 Caucasian |
| Duchenne muscular dystrophy | DMD | 1/3500 male births |
| Hemophilia A | F8 | 1/5000 male births |

### Polygenic Genetic Diseases

- Type 2 diabetes
- Hypertension
- Alzheimer's disease

## Gene Therapy Strategies

### Ex Vivo Gene Therapy

```
Patient cells → Gene editing → Culture in vitro → Infusion back to patient
```

**Representative therapies**: CAR-T, hematopoietic stem cell therapy

### In Vivo Gene Therapy

```
Vector → IV/local injection → Target tissue → Gene expression
```

**Representative therapies**: AAV vector gene therapy

## Clinical Progress

### Approved Therapies

| Therapy | Indication | Efficacy |
|---------|------------|----------|
| Zolgensma | Spinal muscular atrophy | Significantly improved survival |
| Luxturna | RPE65 mutation retinal disease | Vision restoration |
| Glybera | Lipoprotein lipase deficiency | Long-term remission |

### Therapies in Clinical Trials

- **Hemophilia**: Valrox (AAV5-FVIII)
- **Duchenne muscular dystrophy**: SRP-9001
- **Leber's hereditary optic neuropathy**: GZ/SAR402671

## Challenges and Future Directions

### Technical Challenges

- Targeted delivery
- Immunogenicity
- Long-term expression
- Treatment cost

### Future Directions

- Gene editing technology optimization
- In vivo delivery system improvement
- Polygenic disease treatment
