# Gene Therapy Principles

Gene therapy is a technology that treats diseases by delivering normal genes into patient cells, representing a major breakthrough in modern medicine.

## Gene Therapy Strategies

### 1. Gene Addition Therapy

Deliver functional normal genes into cells to compensate for defective gene function.

**Applicable scenarios:**
- Single-gene recessive diseases
- Diseases with insufficient gene expression

### 2. Gene Editing Therapy

Directly repair pathogenic mutations in the patient's genome.

**Technical methods:**
- CRISPR-Cas9
- Base editing
- Prime editing

### 3. Gene Silencing Therapy

Inhibit or suppress expression of abnormal genes.

**Applications:**
- Dominant genetic diseases
- Cancer gene therapy

## Delivery Vectors

### Viral Vectors

| Vector | Characteristics | Applications |
|--------|-----------------|--------------|
| AAV | High safety, high infection efficiency | In vivo gene therapy |
| Adenovirus | High expression efficiency | Cancer therapy |
| Retrovirus | Genome integration | Ex vivo therapy |

### Non-Viral Vectors

- **Lipid nanoparticles (LNP)**
- **Polymer nanoparticles**
- **Electroporation technology**

## Challenges and Future Directions

### Safety Concerns

- Immune responses
- Insertional mutagenesis risk
- Off-target effects

### Future Directions

- Precise in vivo delivery
- Reduce treatment costs
- Expand indication range
