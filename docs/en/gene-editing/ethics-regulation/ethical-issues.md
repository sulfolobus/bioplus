# Gene Editing Ethics

The development of gene editing technology has brought profound ethical challenges that require discussion and consideration from all sectors of society.

## Major Ethical Debates

### 1. Somatic vs Germline Editing

| Type | Scope | Ethical Debate |
|------|-------|----------------|
| Somatic | Only affects the patient | Controllable risks |
| Germline | Inherited by offspring | Cross-generational impact |

### 2. Enhancement vs Treatment

**Therapeutic editing:**
- Treat diseases
- High social acceptance

**Enhancement editing:**
- Improve intelligence
- Select appearance
- Concerns about "designer babies"

## International Positions

### Countries' Attitudes

| Country/Region | Attitude |
|---------------|----------|
| China | Strict regulation, some research allowed |
| United States | Lenient on somatic, strict on germline |
| EU | Strict regulation |

## Ethical Principles

### 1. Informed Consent

- Patients fully understand risks
- Autonomous decision-making

### 2. Fairness Principle

- Does not exacerbate social inequality
- Avoid genetic discrimination

### 3. Transparency Principle

- Research is open and transparent
- Public participation in discussion
