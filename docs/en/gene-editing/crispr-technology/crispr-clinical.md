# CRISPR Clinical Applications

CRISPR gene editing technology is moving from the laboratory to clinical practice. This article introduces the latest progress in medical treatment.

## Approved CRISPR Therapies

### 2023: Casgevy Approval

**Milestone**: World's first approved CRISPR gene editing therapy

| Item | Content |
|------|---------|
| Brand name | Casgevy |
| Generic name | Exagamglogene autotemcel |
| Indication | Sickle cell anemia |
| Developer | Vertex Pharmaceuticals / CRISPR Therapeutics |
| Approval date | December 2023 |

### 2024: Expanded Indications

Casgevy was subsequently approved for **β-thalassemia** treatment.

## Clinical Trial Progress

### Sickle Cell Anemia

**Clinical data:**
- Efficacy rate: Approximately 94% of patients had no pain crisis for at least 12 months
- Durability: Efficacy maintained for 5+ years of follow-up
- Safety: Adverse events manageable

### β-Thalassemia

**Clinical data:**
- Significant reduction in transfusion dependence
- About 90% of patients achieved transfusion independence
- Improved quality of life

## Treatment Process

### Patient Selection

1. Genetic diagnosis confirms mutation type
2. Assess organ function
3. Exclude contraindications

### Treatment Steps

```
Stem cell collection → In vitro gene editing → Preconditioning chemotherapy → Infusion → Monitoring → Follow-up
```

**Time period**: Approximately 2-3 months

## Challenges and Future Directions

### Current Challenges

- **High cost**: Treatment cost approximately $2-3 million per session
- **Accessibility**: Only available at a few medical centers
- **Long-term safety**: More follow-up data needed

### Future Directions

- Reduce treatment costs
- Develop in vivo delivery systems
- Expand indication range
