# Blindness Treatment Research Progress

Blindness refers to partial or complete loss of visual function, including total blindness (no light perception) and low vision (residual vision).

## Types of Blindness

### Total Blindness

- No light perception at all
- May be caused by optic nerve damage, retinal pigmentosa, etc.

### Low Vision

- Residual vision but cannot be corrected with ordinary glasses
- Common in age-related macular degeneration, advanced glaucoma

## Etiology Classification

### Corneal Diseases

| Disease | Characteristics |
|---------|-----------------|
| Corneal Opacity | Corneal scarring |
| Corneal Ulcer | Scarring from infection |
| Keratoconus | Conical corneal protrusion |

### Retinal Diseases

| Disease | Characteristics |
|---------|-----------------|
| Retinitis Pigmentosa | Progressive visual field narrowing |
| Age-related Macular Degeneration | Central vision loss |
| Diabetic Retinopathy | Vision loss from vascular disease |

### Optic Nerve Diseases

| Disease | Characteristics |
|---------|-----------------|
| Glaucoma | Optic nerve atrophy |
| Optic Neuritis | Damage from inflammation |
| Ischemic Optic Neuropathy | Insufficient blood supply |

### Others

- Cataract (curable)
- Eye trauma
- Congenital diseases

## Treatment Progress

### Surgical Treatment

#### Corneal Transplantation

- **Penetrating Keratoplasty**: Full-thickness corneal replacement
- **Lamellar Keratoplasty**: Partial corneal layer replacement
- **Artificial Cornea**: Holocor, BIOKOP

#### Retinal Surgery

- **Vitrectomy**: Retinal detachment treatment
- **Retinal Vascular Surgery**: Diabetic retinopathy
- **Gene Therapy**: Specific hereditary retinal diseases

### Drug Therapy

- Anti-VEGF drugs (macular degeneration, diabetic retinopathy)
- Steroids (uveitis)
- Neurotrophic factors

### Rehabilitation Treatment

#### Low Vision Rehabilitation

- Low vision aids
- Electronic magnification devices
- Visual training

#### Life Skills Training

- Orientation and mobility training
- Self-care training
- Braille learning

## Cutting-edge Technology

### Artificial Vision

#### Retinal Prostheses

| Device | Country | Characteristics |
|--------|---------|-----------------|
| Argus II | USA | 60 electrodes, marketed |
| Alpha-AMS | Germany | 1500 photodiodes |
| PRIMA | France | Miniature wireless |

#### Cortical Visual Prosthesis

- **Orron System**: Visual cortex stimulation
- **BrainGate**: Microelectrode arrays

### Gene Therapy

- **Luxturna**: First gene therapy drug (RPE65 mutation)
- In development: Optogenetic gene therapy

### Stem Cell Therapy

- Retinal pigment epithelium transplantation
- Photoreceptor cell transplantation
- Induced pluripotent stem cell (iPSC)-derived cells

### Tissue Engineering

- Bioengineered cornea
- 3D printed retina
- Artificial lens

### Electronic Navigation

- Ultrasonic guide devices

### Visual Aids

- Electronic readers
- Screen reading software
- Smart glasses

### Living Aids

- Voice devices
- Tactile devices
- Smart home

## Prevention Strategies

### Regular Check-ups

- Routine eye examinations
- Diabetic retinopathy screening
- Glaucoma screening

### Timely Treatment

- Early cataract surgery
- Laser therapy for retinopathy
- Anti-VEGF injections

### Healthy Lifestyle

- Control blood sugar
- Quit smoking
- Protect eyes from UV
- Nutritional supplements (lutein, vitamin A)
