# Bencao Gangmu

Bencao Gangmu (Compendium of Materia Medica) is a monumental work on pharmacology compiled by the Ming Dynasty physician Li Shizhen. It gathers the essence of Chinese materia medica and is known as the "great pharmacological canon of the East."

## Overview

- **Compilation Period**: Ming Dynasty, 24th year of Wanli reign (1578 CE)
- **Author**: Li Shizhen (1518-1593)
- **Status**: Encyclopedia of ancient Chinese pharmacology
- **Scale**: 52 volumes, 1,892 medicinal substances, 11,096 attached formulas

## Main Content

### Drug Classification

Li Shizhen created a classification method from "subtle to gross" and "from lowly to noble":

| Category | Number | Examples |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Herbs | 447 | Ginseng, Gancao, Huangqi |
| Grains | 73 | Rice, Wheat, Millet |
| Vegetables | 105 | Ginger, Garlic, Scallion |
| Fruits | 126 | Peach, Plum, Apricot |
| Wood | 180 | Cypress, Pine, Cassia |
| Insects | 106 | Bees, Scorpions |
| Scales | 92 | Fish, Snakes, Dragons |
| Birds | 76 | Chickens, Ducks, Geese |
| Beasts | 86 | Tigers, Leopards, Oxen |
| Human | 36 | Hair, Nails |

### Drug Documentation

Each medicinal substance includes:

- **Names**: Explanation of name origins
- **Collections**: Description of origin, morphology, collection
- **Properties**: Description of medicinal properties (four natures, five flavors)
- **Indications**: Efficacy and treating diseases
- **Discoveries**: Elaboration of pharmacology and clinical insights
- **Attached Formulas**: Related formulas

### Academic Innovations

1. **Systematic Classification**: Broke the traditional three-grade classification
2. **Textual Correction**: Corrected centuries of drug name confusion
3. **Rich Content**: Added 374 new medicinal substances
4. **Scientific Observation**: Detailed descriptions of drug morphology

## Historical Value

### International Influence

- **Asia**: Spread to Japan, Korea, Vietnam, and other regions
- **Europe**: Introduced to Europe in the 18th century, translated into multiple languages
- **Darwin**: Referenced the book in "On the Origin of Species"

### Academic Status

| Aspect | Contribution |
|--------|---------------|
| Pharmacology | Comprehensive systematic work |
| Botany | Detailed plant morphology and distribution |
| Zoology | Records many medicinal animals |
| Mineralogy | Documents mineral medicines |

## Modern Significance

Bencao Gangmu is not only an important document of TCM but also valuable source material for studying ancient Chinese natural sciences and historical culture. Modern Chinese pharmacology still uses it as a basic reference.
