# Traditional Chinese Medicine Classics

Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) classics are precious cultural heritages of the Chinese nation, carrying thousands of years of deep understanding of life, health, and disease by our ancestors. This section systematically introduces TCM classic works, bringing you to appreciate the wisdom of traditional medicine.

## Classic Works

### Huangdi Neijing

The earliest and most complete medical classic in China, divided into "Suwen" and "Lingshu", laying the theoretical foundation of traditional Chinese medicine.

- **Compilation Period**: Warring States to Western Han
- **Core Ideas**: Yin-Yang, Five Elements, Zang-Fu organs, meridians
- **Main Contribution**: Established the theoretical system of TCM

### Shanghan Lun

Written by Zhang Zhongjing in the Eastern Han, known as the "father of formulas," creating the Six-Meridian syndrome differentiation system.

- **Compilation Period**: Late Eastern Han
- **Core Ideas**: Six-Meridian syndrome differentiation, formula-syndrome correspondence
- **Main Contribution**: Established the foundation of clinical medicine in TCM

### Jingui Yaolue

Another classic by Zhang Zhongjing, focusing on the diagnosis and treatment of internal medicine miscellaneous diseases.

- **Compilation Period**: Late Eastern Han
- **Core Ideas**: Syndrome differentiation and treatment, miscellaneous disease diagnosis
- **Main Contribution**: Created the study of internal medicine miscellaneous diseases in TCM

### Bencao Gangmu

Compiled by Li Shizhen in the Ming Dynasty, gathering the essence of Chinese materia medica.

- **Compilation Period**: Ming Dynasty
- **Core Ideas**: Drug classification, properties and channel entries
- **Main Contribution**: Contains 1,892 medicinal substances, important literature for world pharmacology

### Wisdom of Health Preservation

Summarizing TCM health preservation theories and practical methods, covering diet, daily routine, emotions, exercise, and other aspects.

- **Core Ideas**: Preventive treatment of disease, harmony between man and nature
- **Main Contribution**: Inheriting Chinese health preservation culture
