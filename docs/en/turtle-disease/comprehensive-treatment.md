# Comprehensive Turtle Disease Prevention and Treatment

This document summarizes the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of common turtle diseases, compiled from authoritative turtle breeding and disease control literature.

---

## 1. Common Disease Classification

### 1.1 Infectious Diseases

#### Bacterial Diseases

| Disease | Pathogen | Main Symptoms | Treatment |
|---------|----------|----------------|------------|
| [Pneumonia](bacterial/pneumonia.md) | Bacteria/Virus | Open-mouth breathing, floating | Antibiotics, keep warm |
| [Gastroenteritis](bacterial/gastroenteritis.md) | Bacteria/Parasites | Loss of appetite, abnormal stool | Symptomatic treatment, improve water quality |
| [Septicemia](bacterial/septicemia.md) | Bacteria | Lethargy, weakness, skin bleeding | Antibiotics, fluid therapy |
| [Shell Rot](bacterial/shell-rot.md) | Bacteria/Fungi | Soft shell, rot, foul odor | Clean wound, antibacterial medication |
| [Skin Rot](bacterial/skin-rot.md) | Bacteria | Skin ulceration, white discharge | Disinfect, antibiotic bath |
| [Eye Disease](bacterial/eye-disease.md) | Bacteria | Swollen eyes, discharge | Clean eyes, antibiotic eye drops |

#### Viral Diseases

| Disease | Pathogen | Main Symptoms | Treatment |
|---------|----------|----------------|------------|
| [Herpesvirus](viral/herpesvirus.md) | Herpesvirus | Oral ulcers, nasal discharge, lethargy | Symptomatic, boost immunity |
| [Adenovirus](viral/adenovirus.md) | Adenovirus | Anorexia, weight loss, abnormal stool | Supportive care, boost immunity |
| [Iridovirus](viral/iridovirus.md) | Iridovirus | Skin lesions, lethargy, behavioral changes | Symptomatic treatment |

#### Fungal Diseases

| Disease | Pathogen | Main Symptoms | Treatment |
|---------|----------|----------------|------------|
| [Fungal Dermatitis](fungal/dermatitis.md) | Fungi | White cotton-like material, itching | Antifungal medication, improve environment |
| [Shell Fungus](fungal/shell-fungus.md) | Fungi | White/green spots on shell | Scrape fungus, medication |

### 2. Parasitic Diseases

#### Internal Parasites

| Parasite Type | Transmission | Main Symptoms | Treatment |
|---------------|--------------|----------------|------------|
| [Roundworms](parasites/roundworms.md) | Food, water | Weight loss, appetite loss, worms in stool | Deworming (e.g., Albendazole) |
| [Tapeworms](parasites/tapeworms.md) | Intermediate host | Malnutrition, weight loss | Deworming (e.g., Praziquantel) |
| [Blood Parasites](parasites/blood-parasites.md) | Tick bite | Anemia, lethargy | Deworming, supportive care |
| [Protozoa](parasites/protozoa.md) | Water, food | Diarrhea, weight loss | Anti-protozoal drugs |

#### External Parasites

| Parasite Type | Location | Main Symptoms | Treatment |
|---------------|----------|----------------|------------|
| [Ticks](parasites/ticks.md) | Skin folds | Itching, skin inflammation | Manual removal, insecticide |
| [Mites](parasites/mites.md) | Body | Itching, skin shedding | Bath treatment, insecticide |

### 3. Metabolic Diseases

#### [Soft Shell Disease](other/soft-shell.md)

**Cause**: Calcium or Vitamin D deficiency

**Symptoms**:
- Soft shell, elastic when pressed
- Shell deformation, growth retardation
- Slow movement, difficulty crawling

**Treatment**:
- Supplement calcium (cuttlefish bone, calcium powder)
- Increase UV exposure
- Adjust diet

**Prevention**:
- Maintain adequate calcium intake
- Daily sun exposure
- Regular Vitamin D3 supplementation

#### [Obesity](other/obesity.md)

**Cause**: Overfeeding, lack of exercise

**Symptoms**:
- Fat accumulation in limb pits
- Overweight, difficulty moving
- Fat accumulation in liver area

**Treatment**:
- Control feeding amount
- Increase exercise space
- Adjust diet structure

**Prevention**:
- Feed in moderation
- Provide adequate exercise space

#### [Gout](other/gout.md)

**Cause**: Protein metabolism disorder, dehydration

**Symptoms**:
- Joint swelling, pain
- Stiff limbs, lameness
- Loss of appetite

**Treatment**:
- Diet correction (reduce protein)
- Adequate fluid therapy
- Medication under veterinary guidance

**Prevention**:
- Balanced diet
- Ensure water intake
- Moderate exercise

### 4. Other Common Diseases

#### [Bladder Stones](other/bladder-stones.md)

**Cause**: Dehydration, mineral metabolism disorder

**Symptoms**:
- Difficulty urinating, pain
- Abdominal distension
- Loss of appetite

**Treatment**:
- Surgical removal
- Increase water intake
- Adjust diet

**Prevention**:
- Ensure adequate water intake
- Proper fiber supplementation

#### [Heat Stroke](other/heat-stroke.md)

**Cause**: High temperature, direct sunlight

**Symptoms**:
- Open-mouth breathing, panting
- Lethargy, dehydration
- Death in severe cases

**Treatment**:
- Move to shade
- Replenish water
- Physical cooling

**Prevention**:
- Provide shade facilities
- Maintain suitable temperature
- Ensure ventilation

#### [Frostbite](other/frostbite.md)

**Cause**: Low temperature environment

**Symptoms**:
- Skin purple, swollen
- Stiff limbs
- Difficulty moving

**Treatment**:
- Slow warming
- Antibiotics to prevent infection
- Nutritional support

**Prevention**:
- Keep warm in winter
- Constant temperature breeding

#### Dystocia

**Cause**: Calcium deficiency, physical problems, environmental stress

**Symptoms**:
- Difficulty laying eggs, frequent straining
- Loss of appetite
- Abdominal enlargement

**Treatment**:
- Calcium supplementation
- Provide egg-laying environment
- Veterinary assistance if severe

## 2. Disease Diagnosis Guide

### Appearance Inspection

**Healthy Turtle Characteristics**:
- Shell intact, hard and glossy
- Eyes bright, no discharge
- Nostrils clear, no discharge
- Strong limbs, normal crawling
- Good appetite, normal defecation

**Signs of Illness**:
- Shell soft, rot, spots
- Swollen eyes, discharge
- Skin ulceration, whiteness
- Abnormal behavior, lethargy
- Refusing food, abnormal stool

### Home Preliminary Diagnosis

#### Step 1: Observe Appearance
1. Check shell integrity
2. Check eye brightness
3. Check nostril patency
4. Check skin condition

#### Step 2: Observe Behavior
1. Is it crawling normally?
2. Is appetite good?
3. Is it basking actively?
4. Any abnormal behavior?

#### Step 3: Check Excretion
1. Is stool formed?
2. Is stool color normal?
3. Are there parasites?

## 3. Treatment Protocols

### Common Medications

| Drug Type | Drug Name | Indications | Usage |
|-----------|------------|--------------|-------|
| Antibiotics | Amoxicillin | Bacterial infection | Oral/injection |
| Antibiotics | Cephalosporins | Bacterial infection | Oral/injection |
| Antifungals | Ketoconazole | Fungal infection | Bath/oral |
| Antifungals | Terbinafine | Fungal infection | Topical |
| Deworming | Albendazole | Internal parasites | Oral |
| Deworming | Praziquantel | Tapeworms | Oral |
| Nutrition | Calcium Powder | Calcium deficiency | Mixed with food |
| Nutrition | Vitamins | Nutritional deficiency | Mixed with food/injection |

### Bath Treatment

1. Prepare appropriate size container
2. Prepare solution with correct concentration
3. Place turtle in bath
4. Control appropriate time
5. Remove and dry

## 4. Prevention Measures

### Environment Management

- Keep water clean
- Appropriate temperature and humidity (25-30℃)
- Adequate basking area
- Sufficient UV exposure
- Regular disinfection

### Nutrition Management

- Balanced diet (vegetables + protein)
- Adequate calcium and vitamin supplements
- Avoid overfeeding
- Provide fresh food

### Daily Inspection

- Observe behavioral changes daily
- Regularly check shell condition
- Monitor appetite and defecation
- Regular weighing
