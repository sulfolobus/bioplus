# Protein Functions

Proteins are the main executors of life activities, composed of amino acids, and perform various important functions in living organisms.

## Protein Structure

### Primary Structure

The amino acid sequence determines the basic properties of proteins.

### Secondary Structure

Protein chains fold into α-helices and β-sheets.

### Tertiary Structure

Proteins further fold into three-dimensional structures.

### Quaternary Structure

Multiple protein subunits combine to form functional protein complexes.

## Main Functions of Proteins

### 1. Enzyme Catalysis

- Most enzymes are proteins
- Accelerate biochemical reactions
- Example: digestive enzymes, DNA polymerase

### 2. Structural Support

- Collagen provides tissue structure
- Keratin forms hair and nails

### 3. Transport Function

- Hemoglobin transports oxygen
- Lipoproteins transport fats

### 4. Immune Defense

- Antibodies recognize and eliminate pathogens
- Interferons fight viruses

### 5. Signal Transmission

- Insulin regulates blood sugar
- Receptor proteins receive signals

### 6. Motor Function

- Actin and myosin involved in muscle contraction
- Kinesin involved in intracellular transport

## Proteins and Diseases

| Disease | Protein Problem |
|---------|----------------|
| Alzheimer's Disease | Abnormal beta-amyloid protein |
| Diabetes | Abnormal insulin secretion/function |
| Cystic Fibrosis | CFTR protein functional defect |

## Protein Research Technologies

### Proteomics

Study the structure and function of all proteins in organisms.

### Protein Engineering

Modify proteins to develop new drugs and industrial enzymes.
