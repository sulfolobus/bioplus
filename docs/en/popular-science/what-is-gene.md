# What is a Gene?

A gene is the basic unit of genetic information in living organisms, a specific functional segment on a DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) molecule.

## Basic Concepts of Genes

- **Gene Definition**: Genes are DNA segments that carry genetic information, controlling trait expression in organisms
- **Gene Location**: Genes are located on chromosomes in the cell nucleus
- **Gene Count**: Humans have approximately 20,000-25,000 genes

## Functions of Genes

1. **Storing Genetic Information**
   - Genes carry all instructions needed to build and maintain life
   - Passed from parents to offspring

2. **Controlling Protein Synthesis**
   - Genes guide protein synthesis through transcription and translation
   - Proteins are the main executors of life activities

3. **Determining Biological Traits**
   - Determines eye color, height, blood type, etc.
   - Affects metabolism and disease susceptibility

## Genes and Diseases

Many diseases are related to gene variations:

| Disease Type | Example | Gene Association |
|-------------|---------|------------------|
| Genetic Disease | Cystic Fibrosis | CFTR gene mutation |
| Cancer | Breast Cancer | BRCA1/2 gene mutations |
| Metabolic Disease | Diabetes | Multiple genes work together |

## Frontiers in Gene Research

### Gene Editing Technology

CRISPR-Cas9 technology enables precise gene modification, opening new avenues for treating genetic diseases.

### Gene Sequencing

With technological progress, gene sequencing costs have significantly decreased, making personal genome testing possible.
