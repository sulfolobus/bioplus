# CRISPR: The Story of Genetic Scissors

CRISPR-Cas9 is one of the most important biotechnology breakthroughs in recent years, known as "genetic scissors."

## What is CRISPR?

### Name Origin

CRISPR stands for "Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats."

### Discovery History

- 1987: First discovered in E. coli
- 2005: Found to be related to bacterial immunity
- 2012: Proven to be a gene editing tool
- 2020: Jennifer Doudna and Emmanuelle Charpentier won the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for CRISPR

## How CRISPR Works

### Bacterial Immune System

Bacteria use the CRISPR system to fight viral infections:

1. **Memory**: Store viral DNA fragments as "memory"
2. **Recognition**: Produce RNA to guide Cas proteins to recognize viruses
3. **Cutting**: Cas proteins cut viral DNA

### Gene Editing Applications

```
Guide RNA → Identify target gene → Cas protein cuts → Cell repair → Gene modification
```
