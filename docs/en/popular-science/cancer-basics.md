# How Does Cancer Develop?

Cancer is a general term for diseases characterized by abnormal cell proliferation that may spread to other parts of the body.

## Nature of Cancer

### Normal Cells vs Cancer Cells

| Characteristic | Normal Cells | Cancer Cells |
|----------------|--------------|---------------|
| Growth Pattern | Controlled | Uncontrolled |
| Proliferation Rate | Moderate | Rapid |
| Cell Lifespan | Limited | Unlimited |
| Spread Ability | None | May metastasize |
| Genetic Stability | Stable | Unstable |

## Causes of Cancer

### Gene Mutations

The fundamental cause of cancer is gene mutations that lead to uncontrolled cell growth.

#### Mutation Types

1. **Oncogene Activation**
   - Genes promoting cell growth become overactive
   - Example: RAS, MYC

2. **Tumor Suppressor Gene Inactivation**
   - Growth-inhibiting genes lose function
   - Example: p53, RB

3. **Repair Gene Defects**
   - DNA repair mechanisms fail
   - Example: BRCA1/2

### Carcinogenic Factors

#### External Factors

| Factor | Carcinogenic Mechanism |
|--------|----------------------|
| Tobacco | Induces gene mutations |
| Ultraviolet | DNA damage |
| Viruses | HPV→Cervical cancer, HBV→Liver cancer |
| Chemical Substances | Benzene, nitrosamines |
| Radiation | X-rays, radon |

#### Internal Factors

- Genetic susceptibility
- Immune system function
- Hormone levels
- Chronic inflammation

## Cancer Development Process

### Stages

```
Normal cells → Gene mutation → Precancerous lesions → In situ cancer → Invasive cancer → Metastasis
```

### Hallmarks

1. **Sustained Proliferative Signaling**
2. **Evading Growth Suppressors**
3. **Resisting Cell Death**
4. **Enabling Replicative Immortality**
5. **Inducing Angiogenesis**
6. **Activating Invasion and Metastasis**

## Common Cancer Types

| Cancer | Common Causes | Early Symptoms |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| Lung Cancer | Smoking, air pollution | Persistent cough |
| Breast Cancer | Genetics, hormones | Lump |
| Colorectal Cancer | Diet, genetics | Blood in stool |
| Liver Cancer | Hepatitis, alcohol | Right upper abdominal pain |
| Stomach Cancer | H. pylori | Indigestion |

## Cancer Treatment

### Traditional Treatments

1. **Surgery**: Remove tumor tissue
2. **Chemotherapy**: Use drugs to kill cancer cells
3. **Radiation Therapy**: Use radiation to kill cancer cells

### Modern Treatments

| Method | Principle | Advantages |
|--------|----------|------------|
| Targeted Therapy | Target specific gene mutations | Fewer side effects |
| Immune Checkpoint Inhibitors | Activate immune system | Long-lasting effects |
| CAR-T Cell Therapy | Genetically engineered T cells | Personalized treatment |
| Proton Therapy | Precise radiation | Less damage |

### Treatment Outcomes

- **Early Detection**: High cure rate
- **Middle Stage**: Comprehensive treatment
- **Late Stage**: Extend survival, improve quality of life

## Prevention Strategies

### Primary Prevention

- Quit smoking, limit alcohol
- Healthy diet
- Moderate exercise
- Avoid carcinogens

### Secondary Prevention

- Regular check-ups
- Cancer screening
- Early detection and treatment

### Tertiary Prevention

- Standardized treatment
- Rehabilitation management
- Improve quality of life
