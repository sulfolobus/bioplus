# How Does DNA Work?

DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) is the genetic material of living organisms, carrying all genetic information of an organism.

## DNA Structure

### Double Helix

DNA consists of two antiparallel polynucleotide chains, forming the famous double helix structure.

```
        A ==== T
       /      \
      T        A
       \      /
        G ==== C
       /      \
      C        G
       \      /
        A ==== T
```

### Base Pairing Rules

- **Adenine (A)** pairs with **Thymine (T)**
- **Guanine (G)** pairs with **Cytosine (C)**

## Functions of DNA

### 1. Storing Genetic Information

DNA sequences store all information needed to build and maintain life.

### 2. Replication

DNA accurately replicates before cell division, ensuring genetic information is passed to daughter cells.

### 3. Transcription

Genetic information on DNA is transcribed into mRNA (messenger RNA).

### 4. Translation

Genetic codes on mRNA are translated into protein sequences.

## DNA Replication Process

1. **Unwinding**: Helicase opens the DNA double helix
2. **Primer Synthesis**: Primase synthesizes RNA primers
3. **Extension**: DNA polymerase synthesizes new strands along the template
4. **Ligation**: DNA ligase connects fragments

## DNA and Genes

- **Genes** are functional segments on DNA
- One DNA molecule contains multiple genes
- The human genome has approximately 3 billion base pairs

## DNA Technology Applications

### Gene Sequencing

Reading DNA sequences for disease diagnosis, evolutionary research, etc.

### DNA Identification

For forensic identification, paternity testing, etc.

### Genetic Engineering

Modifying DNA for drug development, genetically modified crops, etc.
