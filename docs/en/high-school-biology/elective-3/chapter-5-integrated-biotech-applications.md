# Chapter 5: Integrated Biotech Applications

## Knowledge Checklist

- Linking genetic, cell, and embryo engineering
- Integrated applications in medicine and agriculture
- Route comparison and optimization
- Ethical, legal, and social implications

## Typical Question

Question: Design a “detection-treatment-follow-up” biotech route for a genetic disease.

How to solve: Organize by objective, method selection, risk control, and outcome evaluation.

## Common Pitfalls

- Listing terms without workflow logic
- Confusing lab feasibility with clinical feasibility
- Ignoring cost and accessibility

1. What is the first step in integrated solution design?
2. Why must ethics be included in technology evaluation?
