# Chapter 5: Scientific Argumentation Training

## Knowledge Checklist

- Structure of evidence-based argument
- Evidence quality and reliability
- Identifying and addressing counterarguments
- Conclusion boundaries and policy suggestions

## Typical Question

Question: Write a balanced argument on expanding clinical gene-editing applications.

How to solve: Use position-evidence-risk-conditional conclusion framework.

## Common Pitfalls

- Claim-first writing without evidence
- Emotional language replacing scientific reasoning
- Conclusions beyond evidence scope

## Self-check Quiz

1. What qualifies as high-quality evidence?
2. How should you respond to a strong opposing view?
3. What elements must a complete argument include?
