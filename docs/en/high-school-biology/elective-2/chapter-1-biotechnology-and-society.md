# Chapter 1: Biotechnology and Social Development

## Knowledge Checklist

- Biotech applications in medicine, agriculture, and environment
- Coexistence of benefits and risks
- Accessibility and equity concerns
- Science communication and public understanding

## Typical Question

Question: Evaluate the value and limits of genetic testing in disease prevention.

How to solve: Build a balanced argument with benefit-risk-privacy-equity dimensions.

## Common Pitfalls

- Listing benefits without risks
- Replacing evidence with opinions
- Ignoring social context differences

## Self-check Quiz

1. Why is risk assessment required for biotech applications?
2. What does accessibility mean in this context?
3. Give one example of two-way interaction between science and society.
